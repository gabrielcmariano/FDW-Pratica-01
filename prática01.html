Astronomy and its branches

I would like to introduce two different branches of Astronomy, and a complement of them stepping ahead to physics itself. The first one we're going to talk about, is Astrophysics.
Astrophysics is, by far, my favorite subject when it comes to the Universe. Why so? Because it's simply astonishing how things work on our immeasurable and beautiful Universe.
So, basically, Astrophysics is the branch of astronomy that employs the principles of physics and chemistry to ascertain the nature of the astronomical objects, rather than their positions or motions in space.

That being said, we can step up to our next subject, which is Cosmology.
Cosmology is a branch of astronomy concerned with the studies of the origin and evolution of the universe, from the Big Bang to today and on into the future.
It is the scientific study of the origin, evolution, and eventual fate of the universe.

Now, to end up our brief speaking, I will explain a little bit of the most known branch of physics that first comes to our mind when we talk about the Universe.
Theoretical physics is a branch of physics that employs mathematical models and abstractions of physical objects and systems to rationalize, explain and predict natural phenomena.
In other words, a theoretical physicist deals all the time with theories that encompass the Universe itself and all its branches.
That was the Isaac Newton, Albert Einstein and nowadays, Michio Kaku's job.
